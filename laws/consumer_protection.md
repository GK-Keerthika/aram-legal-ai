# Consumer Protection Act, 2019

## Purpose
Protects the rights and interests of consumers in India against 
unfair trade practices, defective goods, and deficient services.

## Who is a Consumer?
Anyone who buys goods or avails services for personal use — 
not for resale or commercial purpose.

## Key Consumer Rights
- Right to be protected from harmful goods and services
- Right to be informed about quality, quantity, and price
- Right to choose from a variety of goods at fair prices
- Right to be heard and seek redressal
- Right to consumer education

## Common Situations Covered

### Refund Not Received
You are entitled to a full refund if:
- Product is defective or damaged on arrival
- Service was not delivered as promised
- Seller failed to deliver within agreed time
- Product does not match description
- Order was cancelled but refund not processed

Time limit to claim: Within 2 years of the issue.
First step: Always send written request to seller.
Escalation: consumerhelpline.gov.in or District Commission.

### Defective Product
You can claim any of the following:
- Full replacement of the product
- Free repair within warranty period
- Full refund if repair or replacement not possible
- Compensation for any loss caused by defect

Document everything: Photos, videos, packaging, receipts.
Do not discard defective product before resolution.

### Online Shopping Issues
E-commerce platforms are legally responsible for:
- Ensuring product quality matches listing
- Timely delivery as promised
- Processing refunds within reasonable time
- Handling disputes between buyer and seller

If platform is unresponsive: Escalate to consumerhelpline.gov.in
For suspected fraud: Also report to cybercrime.gov.in

### Service Deficiency
If a service provider fails to deliver as promised:
- You can claim compensation for loss caused
- You can demand redo of service at no extra cost
- You can seek full refund of amount paid
- You can approach consumer commission for damages

Keep all agreements, invoices, and communication records.

## Where to File Complaint

### National Consumer Helpline
- Website: consumerhelpline.gov.in
- Helpline: 1800-11-4000 (toll free)
- Working: Monday to Saturday, 9am to 5pm
- Online filing available 24 hours

### E-Daakhil Portal
- Website: edaakhil.nic.in
- File complaint from home online
- No need to visit office in person
- Track complaint status online

### Consumer Commissions
- District Commission: Claims up to Rs 50 lakhs
- State Commission: Claims Rs 50 lakhs to Rs 2 crores
- National Commission: Claims above Rs 2 crores

### Documents to Collect
- Purchase receipt or invoice
- Order confirmation email or SMS
- Payment proof — screenshot or bank statement
- Product photographs if defective
- All communication with seller — emails, chats, calls

## Important Points
- File complaint within 2 years of issue
- Keep all evidence safely before filing
- Try resolution with seller first — document that attempt
- Mediation is available before formal hearing
- Legal aid available free of cost at District Legal Services
