# Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), 2023

## Purpose
Replaces the Indian Penal Code (IPC) which was 163 years old.
Came into effect on 1st July 2024. Governs criminal offences
and punishments in India with modern provisions.

## Key Differences from IPC
- More victim-friendly provisions
- Faster trial timelines
- Organized crime provisions strengthened
- Terrorism provisions updated
- More clarity on cheating and fraud

## Common Situations Covered

### Cheating
When someone deceives you intentionally to gain money or property.

What qualifies:
- Person made false promises knowing they cannot fulfill them
- Fake business scheme took your money
- Contractor took advance and abandoned work
- Agent or broker disappeared after taking commission
- Fake investment scheme — chit fund, ponzi scheme
- Property fraud — fake documents or title
- Marriage fraud for money or property

Key requirement: Intention to deceive must exist from beginning.
You must show: promise was made, it was false, you suffered loss.

### Criminal Intimidation
Threatening someone with harm to force them into action.

What qualifies:
- Threatening phone calls or messages
- Blackmail — threatening to reveal information
- Extortion — threatening harm unless money paid
- Threatening family members to get compliance
- Physical threats delivered in person or digitally
- Threatening to file false cases
- Rowdy elements threatening business or property

Your right: You do not have to comply with any threat.
Immediate action: File FIR — do not pay or comply.

### Harassment
Repeated unwanted behavior causing distress to a person.

What qualifies:
- Workplace harassment — verbal, physical, or mental
- Neighbour harassment — repeated disturbance
- Domestic harassment — mental torture at home
- Stalking — following or monitoring a person
- Caste or religion based harassment
- Sexual harassment in any location
- Landlord harassment of tenant

Important: Pattern of behavior matters — document every incident.

## Where to File Complaint

### Police Station — FIR
- Visit nearest police station
- Ask to file FIR — First Information Report
- FIR copy is your legal right — demand it
- If police refuses FIR — approach Superintendent of Police
- Zero FIR — can file at any station regardless of location

### Online FIR
- Available in many states through state police websites
- Tamil Nadu: tnpolice.gov.in
- Check your state police website for availability

### Legal Aid — Free Help
- District Legal Services Authority — free legal help
- NALSA helpline: 15100
- Available for all citizens regardless of income
- Free lawyer provided if needed

### Women Specific Helplines
- Women helpline: 1091
- NCW helpline: 7827170170
- One Stop Centre: 181
- Police emergency: 112
