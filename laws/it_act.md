# Information Technology Act, 2000

## Purpose
Provides legal framework for electronic commerce, cyber crime,
and digital communications in India. Amended in 2008 to add
stronger cyber crime provisions.

## Key Offences Covered

### Cyber Fraud
Unauthorized transactions, OTP fraud, phishing, and online scams.

What qualifies:
- Someone called pretending to be bank and got OTP
- Clicked on fake link and money was deducted
- UPI fraud or net banking unauthorized transfer
- Fake customer care number scam
- Investment fraud through online platform

Act fast — golden hour for cyber fraud recovery is first 24 hours.
Every hour delayed reduces chances of money recovery.

### Identity Theft
Using someone's identity, photos, or personal information online
without their consent is a punishable offence.

What qualifies:
- Fake social media profile with your photos
- Someone impersonating you online
- Your Aadhaar or PAN details misused digitally
- Someone opening accounts using your identity
- Morphed photos circulated online

### Hacking
Unauthorized access to any computer, phone, email, or account.

What qualifies:
- Email or social media account taken over
- Phone or laptop accessed without permission
- Password changed by unauthorized person
- Company data accessed without authorization
- Bank app accessed from unknown device

### Online Harassment
Sending threatening, abusive, or obscene content digitally.

What qualifies:
- Repeated abusive messages on any platform
- Threatening messages through WhatsApp or SMS
- Obscene content sent without consent
- Cyber stalking through social media
- Online bullying campaign targeting a person

### Data Misuse
Unauthorized collection, sharing, or misuse of personal data.

What qualifies:
- Personal data sold without consent
- Private photos or videos shared without permission
- Location tracking without knowledge
- Aadhaar data misused by organization

## Where to Report

### National Cyber Crime Portal
- Website: cybercrime.gov.in
- Available 24 hours, 7 days a week
- File complaint from home — no travel needed
- Dedicated sections for financial fraud and other cyber crime

### Cyber Crime Helpline
- Number: 1930
- Available all days including holidays
- Immediate assistance for financial fraud
- Golden hour reporting increases recovery chances

### Local Police Cyber Cell
- Visit nearest police station
- Ask specifically for cyber crime cell
- Bring all printed evidence
- Get written acknowledgement of complaint

## What to Do Immediately

### For Financial Fraud
1. Call your bank helpline immediately — freeze account
2. Call 1930 within the golden hour
3. Screenshot all suspicious transactions and messages
4. File on cybercrime.gov.in within 24 hours
5. Visit police station with printed evidence

### For Account Hacking
1. Change all passwords immediately from safe device
2. Enable two factor authentication on all accounts
3. Check and remove all unknown linked devices
4. Report to platform support team immediately
5. File complaint at cybercrime.gov.in

### For Online Harassment
1. Do not respond to harasser — silence is safer
2. Screenshot everything with timestamps
3. Block and report on the platform
4. File at cybercrime.gov.in
5. Approach police if threats feel physical

## Evidence to Preserve
- Screenshots of messages, profiles, transactions
- Email records with full headers
- Bank statements showing unauthorized transactions
- Call recordings if legally obtained
- URLs of fake profiles or fraudulent websites
- Any communication from fraudster

## Important Points
- Act quickly — especially for financial fraud
- Never delete evidence before filing complaint
- Cyber crime has no geographical boundary in India
- You can file complaint from any state for any state
- Free legal aid available at District Legal Services Authority
